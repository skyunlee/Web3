The llama is a domesticated South American camelid, widely used as a meat and pack animal by Andean cultures since pre-Hispanic times. (--Wikipedia)

Llamas are quiet, gentle and friendly animals. They live with other llamas as a herd. They are also very intelligent and easy to train--they are able to learn simple tasks after a few repititions. In addition, they don't smell bad for outdoor animals!

Llamas and alpacas are very similar to each other. Alpacas are llamas' smaller cousins, bred primarily for fleece.
